USER: As an organizer of this golf league, you need to come up with the roster for all 40 games
between two teams.

  Conditions:
  1. Players from each team get the maximum possibility to play with everyone in the other team
     as well as their own team.
  2. Even for the singles round, try to combine two singles matches into one foursome to create
     more mingling within the 16 people.
  3. Aim is to have that end of the season, everyone competed against many people and played with
     different people within the same team.

  I cannot stress enough on the variability but at the same time, 4 balls, scrambles, alternate
  shots and singles rules need to be strictly followed.

  One of the suggestions for you is to create 10 combinations for 40 matches. For each
  combination, list down for each player how many others will they play/against and see the total
  unique playing opportunities. Out of the 10 combinations, find the combination that provides
  the maximum opportunity.

  Ask me any questions you have and give me your plan before you proceed. When you choose the
  final combination, give me the number of players that everyone will play with in this season.

ASSISTANT:
  Great problem. Before I proceed, a few clarifying questions:

  Questions:

  1. Singles foursomes — what counts as "meeting" someone?
     When two singles matches share a foursome group (e.g., D1 vs R1 + D2 vs R2 walking
     together), does D1 get credit for meeting R2 and D2 even though they aren't directly
     competing? Or only D1's direct opponent R1 counts?

  2. Optimization target — min or average?
     Should I aim to maximise the minimum (so the least-connected player gets the most
     opportunities — fairest outcome), or maximise the total/average across all 16 players?

  3. Partner vs opponent — same weight?
     Playing with someone (same team partner) and playing against someone (opponent) — do both
     count equally as a "unique playing opportunity"?

  My plan once I have your answers:
  - Write a Node.js script that represents the 40-match structure
  - Generate 10 candidate schedules using different rotation and pairing strategies
  - For each schedule, compute a matrix: for every player, how many unique others they played
    with or against
  - Report each schedule's per-player count and total, then select the best
  - Present the winning schedule as the final roster

  Does that approach work, or do you want me to adjust anything?

USER:
  1. D1 get credit for meeting R2 and D2.
  2. Explain the difference between two options. What do you mean by fairest outcome?
  3. Yes, both count equally.

ASSISTANT:
  Good example to illustrate the difference:

  Say we have two candidate schedules and the unique-people-met count per player looks like this:

    Player    Schedule A    Schedule B
    D1        14            12
    D2        14            12
    D3        14            12
    D4        14            12
    D5        14            12
    D6        14            12
    D7        14            12
    D8         6            12
    ...
    Min        6            12
    Total    202           192

  - Maximise total → picks Schedule A (202 > 192), but D8 only met 6 people — they had a very
    poor season experience.
  - Maximise minimum → picks Schedule B (min 12 > 6), because it's fairer — no single player
    gets left behind even if the total is slightly lower.

  In a social league like yours where the whole point is mingling, maximise minimum makes more
  sense. But I wanted you to confirm — do you agree?

  Once you say yes, I'll proceed with the 10-combination analysis.
